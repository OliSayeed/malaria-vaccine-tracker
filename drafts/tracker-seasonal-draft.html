Monthly rate chart (proposed new view): This is where seasonal adjustment becomes visible. The seasonal line oscillates ±40% around the flat line — cases averted per month are highest in Aug–Oct (West/Central African peak season) and lowest in Jan–Feb (dry season). Without adjustment, the model implies that every month of protection is equally valuable; this chart shows that's not true.

This view could be added as a toggle under Trends: "Show monthly rate" alongside the existing cumulative view.
Caveats: Series uses a simplified engine (no dose cascade, no ramp-up). Absolute numbers will differ from the main site; ratios and shapes are indicative. Seasonal profiles are national averages — sub-national variation (especially for DRC, Nigeria, Ethiopia) is not captured. Jan 2024 data gap reflects that most major shipments only began arriving mid-2024.
