Methodology and assumptions

Cost per death averted = (doses needed per child × price per dose) ÷ (vaccine efficacy at year 1 × completion rate for 4 doses × deaths per child at risk per year). The model uses a simplified single-year snapshot: it asks how many deaths would be averted in the first effective year per dollar spent. This intentionally ignores multi-year waning (which the main tracker handles) to give a quick planning estimate.

Coverage gap = Annual births − (total doses shipped or planned ÷ 4). This estimates how many children per year could not be covered by existing supply.

Greedy allocation: The budget allocator sorts countries by cost per death averted (ascending), then allocates funds starting with the most cost-effective country. For each country, it funds up to the coverage gap (unvaccinated children × doses per child × price). Remaining budget rolls to the next country.

Data sources: All data from the Malaria Vaccine Impact Tracker — WHO World Malaria Report 2024, UNICEF Supply Division, UN Population Prospects 2024, Gavi, PMI. Vaccine pricing: R21 $2.99/dose, RTS,S $9.81/dose (Gavi negotiated prices).
